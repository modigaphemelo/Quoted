Narcissism

The self as an endless performance, where others exist only as mirrors

The narcissist doesn't love themselves - they love the image of themselves reflected in your eyes.

For the narcissist, other people are not individuals but functions - sources of supply, validation, or an audience.

Grandiosity and fragility are the same coin. The louder the claim of superiority, the more desperate the need for reassurance.

Narcissism is not self-love. It is a pact with the devil where you trade your soul for a reflection.

The narcissist's tragedy is that they need you to exist, but can never let you be real.

They collect admirers the way misers collect coins, counting them but never spending connection.

When the mask of confidence slips, what's underneath isn't insecurity - it's nothing at all. A void that must be filled immediately with a new performance.

You cannot argue a narcissist into self-awareness. Their mind is a fortress where the moat is filled with your invalidated feelings.

Every conversation is a competition. Every fact you state is a challenge. Every joy you have is a theft from their spotlight.

The need to be right is often just the need to be seen. For the narcissist, being wrong feels like ceasing to exist.

They mistake intensity for intimacy. A dramatic reaction is still supply - indifference is the only insult.

Narcissism is a form of illiteracy - an inability to read the inner world of another person.

The narcissist's greatest fear is not rejection, but ordinariness. To be unseen is to be dead.

They will promise you the world, but only so you'll stay and watch them conquer it.

Your boundaries are not a request to them - they are an act of war.

Apologies are a tool, not a recognition of harm. They say "I'm sorry you feel that way" because your feeling is the problem, not their action.

They don't lack empathy - they selectively deactivate it when it conflicts with their needs.

The past is rewritten nightly to cast them as the hero or the victim - never the perpetrator.

Narcissism is the belief that the world owes you a standing ovation for simply showing up.

They are addicted to the beginning of relationships, where the mirror is still clean and the reflection is kind.

Your suffering is boring to them unless it somehow highlights their own importance or victimhood.

The narcissist doesn't want a partner - they want an audience member with benefits.

They will triangulate, creating drama between people, because a stable peace offers them no position of power.

In their story, they are the lead, the director, and the critic. You are the walk-on role that forgot their lines.

They project their own shame onto you, then punish you for carrying it.

Narcissism is a prison where the inmate is also the warden, and the only visitor is a mirror.

The cruelest trick is the "love bombing" - a preview of a movie that will never be released, only looped in trailers.

They consume your empathy like fuel, and when the tank is empty, they blame you for stalling.

You don't leave a narcissist because you stop loving them. You leave because you realize your love is just another prop in their play.

For the narcissist, attention is oxygen. If they can't get it through admiration, they will get it through chaos.

They live in a hall of mirrors, endlessly chasing a self they can never actually see.

The narcissist's world is small. It contains only them, and the echoes of them bouncing off you.

They mistake your compassion for weakness, your forgiveness for permission, and your departure for betrayal.

The saddest truth is that they are trapped in a cell they decorated themselves, and they call it a palace.

Healing from a narcissist requires you to stop translating their cruelty into a language your heart can accept.

Written by someone who spent too many years translating cruelty into love. You can stop translating now.
