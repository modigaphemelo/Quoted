Purpose

The compass that guides us through the wilderness of existence

The purpose of life is finding the largest burden that you can bear and bearing it.

We act as though comfort and luxury were the chief requirements of life, when all that we need to make us happy is something to be enthusiastic about.

If you plan on being less than you are capable of being, you will probably be unhappy all the days of your life.

When a person can't find a deep sense of meaning, they distract themselves with pleasure.

The high IQ, high openness crowd will spend decades in the wilderness of DIY worldviews and lifestyles only to find out, often too late, that the most meaningful thing to do was just to get married and have kids.

The meaning of life is to find your gift. The purpose of life is to give it away.

He who has a why to live for can bear almost any how.

Purpose is that sense that we are part of something bigger than ourselves, that we are needed, that we have something better ahead to work for.

The two most important days in your life are the day you are born and the day you find out why.

Your purpose is not something you find, but something you build through your choices and actions.

A man without a purpose is like a ship without a rudder.

Purpose is the engine that drives meaningful achievement, not just temporary success.

The mystery of human existence lies not in just staying alive, but in finding something to live for.

Purpose is the antidote to the existential vacuum that plagues modern society.

Your purpose isn't something you discover in a flash of insight; it's something you construct through consistent action.

The purpose of life is not to be happy. It is to be useful, to be honorable, to be compassionate, to have it make some difference that you have lived and lived well.

Purpose is what gives life a narrative arc rather than just a series of random events.

Find purpose in what you do, not in what you get from doing it.

Purpose is the bridge between your present self and your future potential.

The search for purpose is the search for a life worth remembering.

Purpose is not about finding yourself, but about creating yourself in service to something larger.

When you have a strong enough why, you can endure any how.

Purpose is the anchor that keeps you steady when the storms of life rage around you.

The most purposeful lives are those that embrace responsibility rather than avoid it.

Purpose is found in the intersection of your talents, your passions, and what the world needs.

A life without purpose is a story without a plot - full of events but lacking meaning.

Purpose is not a destination to arrive at, but a lens through which to view your journey.

The deepest human need is the need to be needed, to feel that our existence matters.

Purpose is the compass that guides you when the map of life becomes unclear.

Your purpose is not written in the stars, but forged in the choices you make every day.

Purpose is what transforms suffering into sacrifice and obstacles into opportunities.
