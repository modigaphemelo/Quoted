# Quoted - New Topics Reference

## Phase 1: Survival & Daily Function

| # | Topic | HTML Filename | Description |
|---|-------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | The 20-Minute Rule | `the-20-minute-rule.html` | Executive dysfunction fix. You can do anything for 20 minutes. After that, you can stop. Often you won't. The smallest possible action beats the perfect plan. |
| 2 | The Planning Fallacy | `the-planning-fallacy.html` | You systematically underestimate how long things will take. Multiply your time estimate by 2.5. Then add buffer. Then double it again. |
| 3 | Sunk Cost Fallacy | `sunk-cost-fallacy.html` | You stay too long in bad degrees, bad relationships, bad habits because you already invested. What you've already lost is gone. The only question: do you want to lose more? |
| 4 | The Map is Not the Territory | `the-map-is-not-the-territory.html` | Your overthinking problem. The model is not reality. Your understanding of the problem is not the problem. Touch grass. Write one line of code. Pay one bill. |
| 5 | Motivated Reasoning | `motivated-reasoning.html` | You believe what you want to be true. Gambling will pay off. This time they'll change. Your brain is not a truth-seeking machine. It's a comfort-seeking machine. Verify anyway. |
| 6 | Apophenia (False Patterns) | `apophenia.html` | Your pattern-seeking brain sees connections that aren't there. Just because you see a pattern doesn't mean it's real. Test it. Can you predict something new with it? |
| 7 | Gambling & The Illusion of Control | `gambling.html` | The house doesn't win because it's lucky. It wins because you confuse hope with math. The only winning move is not to play. |
| 8 | Enough | `enough.html` | The antidote to "more." You chase more knowledge, more stimulation, more wins and never arrive. The richest person is not the one who has the most. It's the one who needs the least. |

|---|-------|---------------|-------------|

| 12 | The Rescuer Archetype | `rescuer-archetype.html` | The person who "helps" so they can control. Strings attached. They need you broken so they can feel needed. Real help makes itself unnecessary. |

| 18 | Hoovering | `hoovering.html` | Why they suck you back in after you leave. The false promise of change. The late-night text. The emergency that only you can solve. The vacuum wants its supply back. |

| 27 | Leverage | `leverage.html` | Other people's money, time, or code. How to work less by leveraging more. The rich borrow. The poor save. The clever build systems. |

## Phase 4: How Your Own Brain Lies To You

| # | Topic | HTML Filename | Description |
|---|-------|---------------|-------------|
| 28 | Confirmation Bias | `confirmation-bias.html` | You seek evidence for what you already believe. The antidote: seek disconfirming evidence. What would prove you wrong? Go find that. |
| 29 | Dunning-Kruger Effect | `dunning-kruger.html` | The incompetent are confident. The competent doubt themselves. You are likely better than you think. But also worse in areas you haven't tested. |
| 30 | Optimism Bias | `optimism-bias.html` | You believe bad things happen to other people. They don't. Plan for failure. Hope for the best. Prepare for the worst. |
| 31 | Negativity Bias | `negativity-bias.html` | Your brain weighs bad more than good. One criticism cancels ten compliments. Adjust for it. Force yourself to see the good. |
| 32 | Hindsight Bias | `hindsight-bias.html` | "I knew it all along." No you didn't. Keep a prediction log. Write down what you think will happen. Check later. Humility follows. |
| 33 | Fundamental Attribution Error | `fundamental-attribution-error.html` | You judge others by their character, yourself by your circumstances. They're late because they're rude. You're late because traffic. Reverse that. |
| 34 | The Spotlight Effect | `spotlight-effect.html` | You think people are paying attention to you. They're not. They're thinking about themselves. That embarrassing thing you did? No one remembers. |
| 35 | Illusory Superiority | `illusory-superiority.html` | You think you're above average. Most people are wrong. Including you. The average driver thinks they're above average. Do the math. |
| 36 | The Backfire Effect | `backfire-effect.html` | When challenged, you believe your wrong idea more strongly. The antidote: intellectual humility. "I might be wrong. Show me." |
| 37 | Curse of Knowledge | `curse-of-knowledge.html` | Once you know something, you can't imagine not knowing it. You explain poorly because you forget what it's like to be a beginner. Simplify anyway. |
